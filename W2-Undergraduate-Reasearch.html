Alpha-Copter UAV

BSc Graduation Project

BSc Graduation Project “Alpha Copter” is a new form of an Unmanned Aerial Vehicle (UAV), combining a “Quadrotor” and a “Fixed Wing UAV” that was capable of both Conventional, and/or Vertical Take-off and Landing, shorten to (CVTOL UAV). Compared to both fixed wing aircrafts and helicopters, it has achieved longer range, higher speed, less power consumption, higher altitude, increased maneuverable flights, and the ability to land and/or take-off in unprepared regions without the necessity for a run way. Additionally, improving flight safety, by using one of two independent systems in case of one system failure. Its mission objectives were search and rescue, by locating a victim using GPS and digital camera then throwing off the payload as a rescue package.

Alpha Copter was a graduation project that designed and manufactured by team of 8 for the first time in Egypt. It won the best project at the Egyptian Engineering Day (EED), which is the biggest annual competition for students and engineers of Egypt organized by the Institute of Electrical and Electronics Engineers (IEEE)..
